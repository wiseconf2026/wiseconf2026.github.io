Call for Papers

WISE 2026 — Lisbon, Portugal

37th Workshop on Information Systems and Economics  ·  December 16–18, 2026

We are pleased to announce that the 37th Workshop on Information Systems and Economics (WISE) will be held in Lisbon, Portugal, from December 16–18, 2026. WISE is the premier academic forum for research that examines information systems through the lens of economics. Presentations feature work that addresses critical business and societal questions, with a focus on information technology’s role in shaping organizational, market, and societal outcomes.

The workshop focuses on how digital technologies are reshaping economic activity, organizational decision-making, market structures, and society. We invite research that examines the incentives, mechanisms, and consequences associated with the development, deployment, diffusion, and use of information technologies.

More specifically, we encourage submissions in the following thematic areas:

AI, Automation, and Economic Transformation

Papers that examine the economic implications of artificial intelligence, generative AI, agentic systems, automation, and algorithmic decision-making for firms, markets, workers, and consumers.

Digital Platforms, Markets, and Ecosystems

Papers that study digital platforms, online marketplaces, digital goods, ecosystem governance, pricing, competition, business models, and value creation in digital environments.

Data, Analytics, and Digital Experimentation

Papers that investigate the role of data, analytics, machine learning, personalization, recommender systems, and digital experiments in shaping managerial, consumer, and policy decisions.

Trust, Safety, Privacy, and Responsible Technology

Papers that explore cybersecurity, privacy, digital identity, online safety, AI governance, fairness, bias, transparency, accountability, and the regulatory challenges associated with digital technologies.

Work, Organizations, and Human–Technology Interaction

Papers that examine how digital technologies affect work, collaboration, productivity, organizational design, human capital, human–AI collaboration, and the future of digital organizations.

Technology, Society, and Sustainability

Papers that study the broader societal and environmental implications of information technologies, including digital inclusion, healthcare IT, sustainability, misinformation, and the public-policy implications of digital transformation.

FinTech, Digital Currencies, and the Future of Finance

Papers that examine financial technologies, digital payments, cryptocurrencies, stablecoins, central bank digital currencies, tokenization, decentralized finance, and blockchain-based systems, including their implications for market design, financial intermediation, regulation, and access to financial services.

We welcome high-quality theoretical, empirical, experimental, and computational research on these and related topics in the economics of information systems.

Submission Guidelines

Extended abstracts should be no more than 6 pages in length (double-spaced, 12 pt., Times New Roman font, with 1-inch margins). This page limit is inclusive of all text, figures, tables and references. Papers should be submitted in PDF format.

As part of your submission process, you will be asked to indicate the presenting co-author, should the paper be accepted. Additionally, you will be asked to indicate if the work is primarily student-led. Please note that no individual will be scheduled to present more than one paper at the conference.

We encourage submission of papers that would materially benefit from discussant and audience feedback. Accordingly, authors are strongly discouraged from submitting any work that has already received an invitation for revision, been conditionally accepted, or been accepted for publication at a journal at the time of WISE submission. Further, to minimize repeated exposure of the same paper, we also ask that authors not submit work that has been submitted / scheduled for presentation at either ICIS 2026 or WITS 2026.

Finally, authors are discouraged from submitting more than two co-authored works to WISE, particularly if those works do not involve a student co-author. We will consider the number of papers a given individual appears on as co-author when developing the conference program and rendering paper decisions.

WISE does not assume copyright over accepted work and does not publish formal proceedings. Accepted extended abstracts included in the program will be posted unless otherwise requested by the submitting author.
